Neil Postman’s idea in "Amusing Ourselves to Death" was spot on about how entertainment would take over serious discussions, especially through TV and social media. He worried that important issues would get simplified and real information would get lost, which is what we see now with Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube. These platforms often focus more on being entertaining than on providing deep or accurate information. But Postman didn’t fully predict the good side of digital communication. The internet and social media also allow for more detailed and interactive discussions. Podcasts, long articles, and online forums let people dive deeper into complex topics, showing that not all visual media hurt the quality of public conversations.
